I Walked Away From The Bar

"I walked away from the bar and back to myself."

When I first started working as a bartender, it seemed like the perfect job. People, music, and late-night energy were all around me. I told myself that I enjoyed the chaos because it made me feel confident and enjoyable. Beneath the small talk and jokes, though, I was using my work as a means of avoiding my personal life. If I was always working, always pouring drinks, I didn't have to think about how anxious and disconnected I really felt.

The next day I phoned my doctor and told a close friend the truth. Speaking the words aloud scared me - yet it also brought relief. I began treatment for substance use plus for anxiety. With help from professionals and from friends, I chose to quit the bar job. The first months were hard. I had to learn again how to relax when no glass sat in my hand but also how to face stress without running away.
